## Rationale

### 1. Haven't Earned the Price Yet

**The value moment happens AFTER someone uses the product:**
> "Oh, this explains things I didn't realize I didn't understand."

Right now, visitors haven't had that moment yet.

**Pricing before value = mental friction.**

### 2. Still Validating Mental Models

**Open questions we'll answer with free tier:**
- Do users think of this as "insurance-like" or "software-like"?
- Do they expect monthly, quarterly, or "whenever I want"?
- Do they mentally anchor it to $10, $30, or $100?

**Publishing pricing now locks us into assumptions we don't need to make yet.**

### 3. Early Pricing Scares Off Right Users

**Owner/CFO/Ops reaction patterns:**
- If price shown early â†’ "I'll decide later" (postponement)
- If price absent but reasonable â†’ "I'll see what this is" (curiosity)

**For a clarity product, curiosity is our strongest lever.**

**Publish after one of these is true:**
- 10-20 people have used the free snapshot

**At that point, pricing becomes obvious instead of theoretical.**
