## Content Quality Notes

### What Works Well
- **Analogies**: Phone book for DNS, signed documents for DKIM, letterhead for SPF
- **Empathy**: "You nod along in meetings but don't understand" - acknowledges real frustrations
- **Practicality**: Specific checks readers can do themselves (visit website, test email, check padlock)
- **Non-condescending**: Respects that readers are smart but not technical
- **Actionable**: Clear next steps, checklists, verification processes

### Consistent Elements
- Problem acknowledgment in opening
- Plain English explanations
- Business impact focus (not technical details)
- "What can go wrong" sections
- "Questions to ask" sections
- Verification/testing guidance
- Subtle service reference at end

### Voice & Tone
- Conversational but professional
- Direct and honest
- Occasionally uses "you probably..." or "most business owners..." to normalize concerns
- No emojis, no excessive enthusiasm
- Respectful of reader's intelligence and time
