### Common Questions & How to Answer

**Q: "How is this different from what my MSP does?"**
A: "We're not replacing your MSP‚Äîwe're giving you independent visibility into what they're managing. Think of it like getting a second opinion, except we're not making medical decisions, just helping you understand what you have."

**Q: "Is this a security tool?"**
A: "No, we're not a security scanner or monitoring service. We explain what your current setup looks like from the outside, in language you can understand. We don't send alerts, scan for vulnerabilities, or tell you what to fix."

**Q: "Do you access our network?"**
A: "No. We only look at publicly available information‚Äîthe same things anyone on the internet could see (your domain registration, email security records, SSL certificates, tech stack). We never access your internal network or systems."

**Q: "What's the ROI?"**
A: "We're $19.99/month‚Äîless than an hour of your MSP's time. The ROI comes from avoiding surprises, being prepared when questions come up, and having an independent check on what you're being told about your IT."

**Q: "Can you tell me what to fix?"**
A: "We explain what we observe and what assumptions your setup is making, but we don't make recommendations or tell you what to fix. That's a conversation for you and your IT provider. We give you the clarity to have that conversation effectively."

**Q: "Will this work with our compliance needs?"**
A: "We create dated records in plain English that can support compliance documentation, but we're not a compliance tool. We explain what exists, not whether it meets specific standards."

**Q: "What if we find something concerning?"**
A: "Our reports use calm, factual language. We don't raise alarms or use fear tactics. We explain what we observe, what it means in business terms, and what assumptions are being made. Then you decide what (if anything) to do about it."

## üìä Use Cases & Stories

### Use Case 1: Insurance Renewal
**Scenario:** Business owner gets cyber insurance questionnaire asking about email security (SPF, DKIM, DMARC). They have no idea.

**How We Help:** Our report explains exactly what they have configured, in language they can understand. They can answer insurance questions confidently or forward the report to their insurance agent.

### Use Case 2: MSP Transition
**Scenario:** Business owner is unhappy with current MSP and interviewing new ones. Everyone says different things about what's currently configured.

**How We Help:** Independent dated record shows exactly what the setup looked like before the transition. New MSP can see what they're inheriting. No "he said, she said."

### Use Case 3: Inherited IT Setup
**Scenario:** New business owner bought a company and has no idea what IT infrastructure they inherited. Previous owner isn't being helpful.

**How We Help:** Get a baseline understanding of what exists, what's assumed, and what questions to ask. No longer flying blind.

### Use Case 4: Quiet IT Changes
**Scenario:** SSL certificate expired without warning. Business owner didn't know it was expiring, didn't know who was responsible for renewal.

**How We Help:** Monthly snapshots show certificate expiry dates, create a record of who was responsible, and help avoid future surprises.

### Use Case 5: Due Diligence
**Scenario:** Investor or buyer wants to understand IT infrastructure as part of due diligence.

**How We Help:** Dated records in business language that non-technical people can review. Shows configuration history, not just current state.

## üìà Lead Qualification Questions

Ask these to qualify leads before heavy follow-up:

1. **"Do you currently have someone managing your IT?"** (If no, they need an MSP first)
2. **"How many domains does your business operate?"** (Helps gauge fit and pricing)
3. **"Have you recently dealt with insurance, audits, or due diligence questions about IT?"** (High-intent trigger)
4. **"Do you feel like you understand your current IT setup?"** (Pain point awareness)
5. **"When IT questions come up, do you have documented answers?"** (Problem recognition)

### Red Flags (Disqualify):
- They want security scanning or monitoring
- They're looking to replace their IT provider
- They're a tech company with internal IT expertise
- They want recommendations or remediation
- They have no IT budget whatsoever
- They're expecting 24/7 support

### Green Flags (High Priority):
- They've recently had IT questions come up and couldn't answer
- They're preparing for insurance, audit, or M&A
- They've recently hired/lost IT staff
- They're evaluating or transitioning MSPs
- They're non-technical but have IT questions regularly
- They mention wanting "peace of mind" or "independent view"
