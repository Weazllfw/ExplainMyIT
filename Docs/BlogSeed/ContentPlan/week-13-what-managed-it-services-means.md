# What 'Managed IT Services' Actually Means (And What It Doesn't)

"We provide managed IT services" is something you hear constantly. But what does it actually mean?

Every IT provider uses this term, but they all mean something different. One company's "managed services" is another's "basic monitoring." And you're left wondering what you're actually paying for.

Here's what managed IT services really means â€” what's typically included, what costs extra, and how to decide if you need it.

---

## What Is "Managed IT Services" (Simple Definition)?

**Traditional IT**: You call when something breaks. They fix it. You pay per incident or hourly.

**Managed IT Services**: They proactively monitor and maintain your systems. You pay a flat monthly fee for defined services.

**The key difference**: Managed = proactive. Traditional = reactive.

---

## What's Typically Included in "Managed IT"

### 1. **Monitoring**
**What it means**: They watch your systems 24/7 for problems

**What they monitor**:
- Servers (if you have them)
- Network performance
- Disk space usage
- Security alerts
- Software updates needed

**What this prevents**: Issues caught before they cause downtime

---

### 2. **Remote Support**
**What it means**: Help desk support via phone/email/chat

**Typical response times**:
- Critical issues: 1-4 hours
- Standard issues: 4-24 hours
- Questions/requests: 24-48 hours

**Usually included**: Unlimited tickets (within reason)

---

### 3. **Software Updates and Patches**
**What it means**: They handle security updates and patches

**What they update**:
- Operating systems
- Business software
- Security software
- Server software (if applicable)

**What this prevents**: Security vulnerabilities from outdated software

---

### 4. **Backup Management**
**What it means**: They configure, monitor, and test backups

**What's included**:
- Backup configuration
- Monitoring backup success/failure
- Alerts if backups fail
- Periodic restore testing

**What's usually NOT included**: Backup storage costs (you pay separately)

---

### 5. **Basic Security**
**What it means**: Standard security measures

**Usually includes**:
- Antivirus/antimalware
- Firewall management
- Security patch management
- Basic threat monitoring

**Usually doesn't include**: Advanced security, penetration testing, compliance audits

---

### 6. **User Management**
**What it means**: Adding/removing employee accounts

**Includes**:
- Creating new user accounts
- Setting up email
- Removing access when employees leave
- Password resets

---

## What's Usually NOT Included (And Costs Extra)

### 1. **On-Site Visits**
**Reality**: "Managed" usually means remote. On-site costs extra.

**When you need on-site**: Hardware issues, networking setup, equipment installation

**Extra cost**: $100-200/hour or per-incident fee

---

### 2. **Hardware Purchases**
**Reality**: They manage your IT, but you buy the equipment

**What you pay for**: Computers, servers, networking equipment, printers

**What they do**: Recommend, configure, and support hardware (but you purchase it)

---

### 3. **New Projects**
**Reality**: Ongoing maintenance is covered. New implementations cost extra.

**Examples of "projects"**:
- Website redesign
- New software implementation
- Office network overhaul
- Cloud migration

**How it's billed**: Separate project fee or hourly rate

---

### 4. **Training**
**Reality**: They'll help users with problems. Formal training costs extra.

**What's included**: "How do I do X?" questions

**What costs extra**: Formal training sessions, documentation creation, onboarding programs

---

### 5. **Phone System**
**Reality**: Some MSPs handle phone systems. Many don't.

**Check**: Ask explicitly if VoIP/phone system is included

---

### 6. **Specialized Software Support**
**Reality**: They support common business software. Industry-specific software is extra or not supported.

**Usually supported**: Microsoft Office, email, common tools

**Usually not supported**: Custom software, specialized industry tools

---

## Different Levels of "Managed"

### Basic Monitoring (Sometimes Called "Managed")
**What it includes**:
- Server/network monitoring
- Alerts when problems detected
- You still have to call when issues arise

**Monthly cost**: $50-150 per device/user

**Good for**: Businesses with some IT knowledge who just need early warnings

---

### Standard Managed Services
**What it includes**:
- Monitoring
- Remote support
- Software updates
- Backup management
- User management

**Monthly cost**: $100-250 per device/user

**Good for**: Most small to medium businesses

---

### Fully Managed / White Glove
**What it includes**:
- Everything in standard
- Proactive optimization
- Strategic planning
- Dedicated account manager
- Faster response times
- Some on-site included

**Monthly cost**: $200-500+ per device/user

**Good for**: Businesses who want IT completely off their plate

---

## Managed IT vs. Other IT Service Models

### Break-Fix Model
**How it works**: You call when something breaks. They fix it. You pay per incident.

**Pros**: No monthly fee. Only pay when you need help.

**Cons**: No proactive support. Issues surprise you. Can get expensive fast.

**Cost**: $100-200/hour, typically

---

### Managed Services Model
**How it works**: Flat monthly fee for defined services. Proactive monitoring and maintenance.

**Pros**: Predictable costs. Fewer emergencies. Proactive issue prevention.

**Cons**: Monthly fee even if nothing breaks. May pay for services you don't use.

**Cost**: $100-250/device/user per month

[See examples](/examples) of what managed IT actually monitors and maintains on a day-to-day basis.

---

### In-House IT
**How it works**: You employ IT staff directly.

**Pros**: Dedicated to your business. Deep knowledge of your systems. Immediate availability.

**Cons**: Expensive (salary + benefits + training). Limited expertise (one person can't know everything).

**Cost**: $50,000-100,000+ per year for one person

---

### Hybrid Model
**How it works**: Basic in-house person + MSP for specialized needs or overflow

**Pros**: Daily support in-house, expert help when needed

**Cons**: Coordination between internal and external

**Cost**: In-house salary + reduced MSP fees

---

## How MSPs Typically Price Their Services

### Per-Device Model
**How it works**: $X per computer/device managed

**Example**: $100/month per workstation, $250/month per server

**Pros**: Simple, scales with business

**Cons**: Can get expensive with lots of devices

---

### Per-User Model
**How it works**: $X per employee

**Example**: $150/month per user (covers their computer, email, phone, etc.)

**Pros**: Simple to understand

**Cons**: Doesn't account for server-heavy businesses

---

### Flat-Rate Model
**How it works**: One price for everything, regardless of devices/users

**Example**: $2,000/month for up to 25 users

**Pros**: Very predictable

**Cons**: Might overpay if you're small, underpay if you're large (and MSP might cut corners)

---

### Tiered Packages
**How it works**: Basic/Standard/Premium packages with different features

**Example**:
- Basic: $100/user (monitoring, remote support)
- Standard: $150/user (adds backups, security)
- Premium: $250/user (adds strategic planning, faster response)

**Pros**: Choose what you need

**Cons**: Can be confusing what's in each tier

---

## Questions to Ask Before Hiring an MSP

### About Services

1. **What exactly is included in your monthly fee?**
2. **What's NOT included that costs extra?**
3. **What are your response time commitments?**
4. **Do you provide 24/7 support or business hours only?**
5. **How do you handle after-hours emergencies?**

---

### About Coverage

6. **How many devices/users does your price cover?**
7. **Is on-site support included or extra?**
8. **Do you support our specific software?** (List your critical software)
9. **Do you cover hardware issues or just software?**

---

### About the Relationship

10. **Who will be our main point of contact?**
11. **How do we submit tickets or requests?**
12. **How often will we meet for reviews?**
13. **What reports do you provide?**
14. **What's your contract length and cancellation policy?**

---

### About Track Record

15. **Can you provide references from similar businesses?**
16. **What certifications do your technicians have?**
17. **How long have you been in business?**
18. **What happens if our main contact leaves your company?**

---

## Red Flags When Evaluating MSPs

### "Unlimited Everything"
**Why it's a red flag**: Nothing is truly unlimited. Read the fine print.

**What to look for**: Reasonable use policies, exclusions

---

### Extremely Low Pricing
**Why it's a red flag**: You get what you pay for. If price is 50% below market, corners are being cut.

**What to ask**: What's included at that price? How do you keep costs so low?

---

### No Written SLA
**Why it's a red flag**: Without a service level agreement, you have no recourse for poor service.

**What to demand**: Written SLA with response times and uptime guarantees

---

### Long-Term Contracts with No Escape
**Why it's a red flag**: Good MSPs earn your business monthly. Bad MSPs trap you.

**What to negotiate**: Month-to-month or annual contract with reasonable termination clause

---

### They Don't Ask Questions
**Why it's a red flag**: How can they quote you accurately without understanding your needs?

**What to expect**: Discovery call, questions about your systems and goals, tailored proposal

## When Managed IT Makes Sense

### You Should Consider Managed IT If:
- You have 5+ employees
- You're spending >10 hours/month on IT issues
- You've had multiple unexpected IT emergencies
- Your current IT is reactive, not proactive
- You want predictable IT costs
- You don't have (and don't want) in-house IT staff

---

### Managed IT Might Not Make Sense If:
- You're a solo business with simple IT needs
- You have capable in-house IT staff
- You rarely have IT issues
- Your IT is already stable and well-maintained
- You prefer to pay only when you need help

---

## What Good Managed IT Actually Looks Like

### Proactive
- They catch issues before you notice
- Regular maintenance prevents problems
- You hear from them before things break

---

### Communicative
- Monthly reports on system health
- Quarterly business reviews
- They explain things in plain English
- Fast response to tickets

---

### Strategic
- They recommend improvements
- They plan for your growth
- They align IT with your business goals
- They're a partner, not just a vendor

---

### Transparent
- Clear invoicing
- No surprise charges
- Honest about limitations
- Admit when something is outside their expertise

---

## Bottom Line: Is Managed IT Worth It?

**Managed IT is worth it if**:
- Monthly cost < cost of frequent emergencies + your time
- You value predictability over pay-as-you-go
- You want proactive support, not reactive firefighting

**It's not worth it if**:
- You rarely have IT issues
- You prefer to pay only when you need help
- You have capable in-house IT already

**Average breakeven**: If you're spending 10+ hours/month on IT issues OR paying $200+/month in break-fix fees, managed probably makes sense.

**Don't choose based on price alone**: Cheapest MSP is rarely best MSP. Choose based on service quality, communication, and fit.
