# Emergency IT Contact List: What to Prepare Now (Before You Need It)

It's 2 AM. Your website is down. Or your email isn't working. Or your domain expired. Or you're locked out of critical systems.

Who do you call?

Most business owners don't know until they're in crisis mode â€” frantically Googling, digging through old emails, trying phone numbers that don't work anymore.

Here's what to document *now* so you're not scrambling during an IT emergency.

---

## Why "I'll Figure It Out When It Happens" Doesn't Work

**In an IT emergency**:
- You're stressed and not thinking clearly
- Every minute of downtime costs money
- Old contact info might be outdated
- You waste time hunting for information
- Some issues have time-sensitive fixes

**With a prepared contact list**:
- You know exactly who to call
- You have all necessary account info
- You can act immediately
- You reduce downtime
- You stay calm(er)

**Time to create list**: 1-2 hours  
**Time saved during emergency**: Hours to days

---

## What to Include in Your IT Emergency Contact List

### 1. Domain Registrar

**Why you need it**: Domain expiration or DNS issues

**Information to document**:
- Company name
- Website/login URL
- Account number or customer ID
- Phone support number
- Email support
- Account login (store in password manager)
- Current domain expiration dates

**Example**:
```
Domain Registrar: Namecheap
Website: namecheap.com/myaccount
Support: 1-888-XXX-XXXX
Email: support@namecheap.com
Account: [in password manager]
Domains: example.com (exp 2027-03-15)
```

---

### 2. Website Hosting Provider

**Why you need it**: Site down, server issues, backup restoration

**Information to document**:
- Company name
- Website/login URL
- Account number
- Support phone (regular and emergency)
- Support email
- Live chat option (if available)
- Account login (store in password manager)
- Server IP address or details

**Example**:
```
Hosting: Bluehost
Website: bluehost.com/hosting
Support: 1-888-XXX-XXXX (24/7)
Emergency: Same number, say "emergency"
Account: [in password manager]
Server: IP 123.45.67.89
```

---

### 3. Email Provider

**Why you need it**: Email not working, deliverability issues

**Information to document**:
- Service name (Google Workspace, Microsoft 365, etc.)
- Admin console URL
- Admin account login
- Support contact
- Number of accounts/licenses

**Example**:
```
Email: Google Workspace
Admin: admin.google.com
Account: admin@example.com [in password manager]
Support: https://support.google.com/a
Licenses: 10 users
```

---

### 4. IT Support Provider (If You Have One)

**Why you need it**: General IT issues, server problems

**Information to document**:
- Company name
- Primary contact name
- Phone (regular hours and after-hours)
- Email
- Emergency escalation process
- Service agreement details

**Example**:
```
IT Support: ABC Tech Solutions
Contact: John Smith
Phone: 1-555-XXX-XXXX (M-F 9-5)
Emergency: 1-555-XXX-XXXX ext 999 (24/7)
Email: support@abctech.com
Contract: Monthly retainer, 4-hour response
```

---

### 5. Web Developer/Contractor

**Why you need it**: Website broken, need urgent changes. Emergencies often happen during [vendor transitions](/use-cases#msp-transition) when knowledge hasn't transferred properly.

**Information to document**:
- Name/company
- Phone
- Email
- Availability (hours, emergency contact?)
- Hourly rate
- What they have access to

**Example**:
```
Developer: Jane Doe Web Design
Phone: 1-555-XXX-XXXX
Email: jane@example.com
Available: M-F 10-6, emergency add $150/hr
Hourly rate: $100
Access: Hosting, WordPress admin
```

---

### 6. Payment/Billing Contacts

**Why you need it**: Update payment methods, resolve billing issues

**Information to document**:
- Credit card on file for each service
- Billing contact person
- Invoice email address
- Accounting system details

**Example**:
```
Hosting billing: Visa ending 1234 (exp 12/27)
Domain billing: Mastercard ending 5678 (exp 06/26)
Billing contact: billing@example.com
```

---

### 7. SSL Certificate Provider

**Why you need it**: Certificate expired or errors

**Information to document**:
- Where SSL is managed (often hosting provider)
- Certificate type (Let's Encrypt, paid, etc.)
- Expiration date
- Renewal process

**Example**:
```
SSL: Let's Encrypt (via Bluehost)
Auto-renews: Yes
Expires: 2026-06-15
Managed by: Hosting provider
```

---

### 8. Backup System

**Why you need it**: Need to restore from backup

**Information to document**:
- Where backups are stored
- How to access them
- How to restore
- Last verified backup date

**Example**:
```
Backups: UpdraftPlus plugin
Storage: Google Drive (backups@example.com)
Frequency: Daily
Last tested: 2026-01-15
Restoration guide: [link to doc]
```

---

### 9. Third-Party Services

**Why you need it**: Integration failures, service disruptions

**Information to document** (for each service):
- Service name
- Account login
- Support contact
- API keys (if applicable)
- What it integrates with

**Example**:
```
Email marketing: Mailchimp
Login: [in password manager]
Support: mailchimp.com/contact
Integrates with: Website signup forms
```

---

### 10. Security/Monitoring Services

**Why you need it**: Security alerts, monitoring issues

**Information to document**:
- Service name (Sucuri, WordFence, etc.)
- Login
- Alert email/phone
- Support contact

## Common Emergency Scenarios and Who to Call

### Website Is Down
**Call first**: Hosting provider  
**Why**: They control the server  
**Backup**: Web developer (if hosting says it's not their issue)

---

### Email Not Working
**Call first**: Email provider  
**Why**: They control email service  
**Check**: MX records haven't changed unexpectedly

---

### Domain Expired
**Call first**: Domain registrar  
**Why**: Only they can renew/restore  
**Urgent**: Redemption period is time-sensitive

---

### Site Hacked
**Call first**: Hosting provider (to take site offline)  
**Call second**: Security service or web developer (to clean)  
**Call third**: IT support (to harden security)

---

### SSL Certificate Error
**Call first**: Hosting provider (if they manage SSL)  
**Why**: Usually quick fix on their end  
**Backup**: Web developer if custom SSL

---

### Locked Out of Admin
**Call first**: Web developer (to reset)  
**Why**: They have backup access  
**Backup**: Hosting provider (database access for reset)

---

## How to Store This Information Securely

### Use a Password Manager
**Recommended**: 1Password, LastPass, Bitwarden

**Why**:
- Encrypted and secure
- Accessible from anywhere
- Can be shared with trusted team members
- Easy to update

**How to organize**:
- Create folder: "IT Emergency Contacts"
- Store each contact as a "Secure Note"
- Include all relevant info
- Add actual credentials as separate login entries

---

### Backup: Physical Document
**Why**: If you can't access password manager (unlikely but possible)

**What to document**:
- Contact names and phone numbers
- URLs and account identifiers
- Emergency procedures

**What NOT to document on paper**: Actual passwords (security risk)

**Where to store**: Locked safe, file cabinet, known location

---

### Share with Key People
**Who should have access**:
- Business owner
- Backup person (manager, partner)
- Trusted admin

**How to share**:
- Through password manager sharing
- Or physical copy in secure location
- Not via email or unencrypted files

## Testing Your Emergency Plan

**Schedule a quarterly drill**:

**Test 1: Website Recovery**
- Can you access hosting control panel?
- Can you find backup files?
- Do you know how to restore?

**Test 2: Contact Verification**
- Call each support number
- Verify they're still correct
- Confirm account numbers work

**Test 3: Access Verification**
- Log into each critical system
- Confirm passwords are current
- Verify two-factor auth works

**Test 4: Team Communication**
- Does your backup person have access?
- Can they find the emergency list?
- Do they know the procedures?

## Red Flags: You're Not Prepared

### "I Think the Number Is..."
**Fix**: Document exact numbers now, don't rely on memory

---

### "It's Probably in an Old Email Somewhere..."
**Fix**: Search now, document, don't wait for emergency

---

### "Only One Person Knows How to Fix This"
**Fix**: Document their process, create backup contact

---

### "We'll Google It If Something Goes Wrong"
**Fix**: Google during a crisis wastes time. Document now.

---

### "I Haven't Tested Backups in Years"
**Fix**: Test restoration this week

## What to Do in an Actual Emergency

### Step 1: Don't Panic
- Stressed decisions are bad decisions
- Take 2 minutes to breathe and think

---

### Step 2: Assess the Situation
- What exactly isn't working?
- When did it start?
- Did anything change recently?
- Is it affecting customers?

---

### Step 3: Consult Your Contact List
- Who handles this specific issue?
- What information will they need?
- What's the escalation path?

---

### Step 4: Make the Call
- Be clear about the issue
- Provide account/system details
- Ask for estimated resolution time
- Get ticket/case number

---

### Step 5: Document Everything
- What happened
- Who you called
- What they did
- How it was resolved
- When it was resolved

**Why**: If it happens again, you'll know the fix

---

## Update Schedule

**Monthly**:
- Review for outdated phone numbers or contacts
- Verify critical systems still accessible

**Quarterly**:
- Test emergency procedures
- Practice backup restoration
- Verify support contacts still work

**Annually**:
- Full audit of all information
- Update any changed services or providers
- Review emergency procedures with team

---

## Special Situations

### Remote/After-Hours
**Extra info needed**:
- VPN access (if required for some systems)
- Phone numbers that work outside business hours
- Backup contacts if primary is unavailable

---

### Multiple Team Members
**Document who's responsible for what**:
- Primary contact for each system
- Backup contact if primary unavailable
- Who has authority to authorize emergency spending

---

### Seasonal Businesses
**Plan for off-season**:
- Who monitors systems when business is closed?
- How to reach key people during off-season?
- Automated monitoring alerts
